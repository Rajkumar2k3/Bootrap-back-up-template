About Staff Selection Commission (SSC)

Staff Selection Commission Recruitment (SSC) is the authorized organization under Government of India, which is responsible to recruit staffs for various posts in the various Ministries and Departments of Government of India and Subordinate Offices.

Staff selection Commission Recruitment (SSC) was founded 40 years ago on 4th November, 1975 and it was formerly known as Subordinate Service Commission till 26th September, 1977. The Headquarters of Staff Selection Commission (SSC) is located in New Delhi and it has 7 Regional Offices and 2 Sub-Regional Offices that spread across throughout the Country.

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) is an attached office of the Department of Personal and Training. It comprises of a Chairman, 2 Members and a Secretary-cum-Controller of Examinations who are appointed on such terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the Union Government from time to time. The Commission is provided such supporting staffs as considered by the Union Government.

All Examinations as well as administration matters are submitted to the chairman by 2 members. The Secretary acts under both the members. Besides, there are post of 1 Director, 1 Deputy Secretary, 2 Joint Directors, 9 Under Secretaries, 4 Deputy Directors, 1 Finance & Budget Officers, 1 Assistant Director, 24 Sections Officers and more than 183 Supporting Officers at the Head Quarters for discharging the duties and responsibilities of the commission.

Regional & Sub-Regional Offices of Staff Selection Commission

For smooth conduct of examination through a large network of examinations centers/sub-centers situated in different parts of the country for the convenience of the candidates, the commission has provided the regional setup.
